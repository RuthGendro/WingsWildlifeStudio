Field Care

  • A good rule of thumb is to treat your bird in the field as if it's already been mounted. Be gentle. We love our dogs, but even the softest-mouthed dog can create damage, so if possible, try to retrieve the bird before the dog.
  • DO NOT wring the neck of your bird. If bird is wounded, firmly squeeze the chest under the wings (armpits), until bird expires.
  • Do not field-dress/gut your bird.
  • Do not tuck the head under a wing.
  • Try to keep a gallon ziplock or bread bag with you when you hunt and place in the bag, then your game bag, however, it's best to carry the bird by the feet. I prefer you DON'T use a nylon stocking.
  • NEVER wrap your bird in newspaper of any kind, the newsprint can bleed on feathers.
  • Wipe excess blood off with a wet cloth or paper towel. Use only water, even creek water is fine.
  • Wrap bird's head in paper towel and fold over the bird's back (see photo) and freeze in double ziplock bag.

If you already have a bird in the freezer and have not followed these instructions to the letter, that's fine, but we will need to assess specimen after it has been thawed.

Delivery of Bird

  • Deliver fresh if possible (if harvested the same day), otherwise freeze as indicated.
  • If you need to ship a bird, call for specific instructions and shipping information. All shipping fees are in addition to the price of mount and paid by customer.

Tags: Important Information!

  • Federal Fish and Wildlife Services REQUIRE a completed tag accompany your bird. We CANNOT accept any bird without a SIGNED and COMPLETELY filled tag/s. Tag numbers are required for wild turkeys, sea ducks and some exotics (in addition to WILD ID #).
